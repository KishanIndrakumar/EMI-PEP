TIG — Human Tetanus Immunoglobulin
Standard: 250 IU IM anterolateral thigh | Increase to 500 IU if: infected wound, deep/contaminated, tissue necrosis, burns, large adult, surgical delay >24h
Give at SEPARATE site to vaccine. Bioavailable 2–3 days after IM.
Tetanus Vaccine (0.5ml IM)
Under 10 years: DTaP/IPV (or Tdap/IPV if shortage) | 10 years and over: Td, Tdap, Td/IPV or Tdap/IPV
Booster every 10 years for fully vaccinated adults. Store +2 to +8°C. Do not use if frozen.

HIV PEP Drugs, Doses & Side Effects — Table 11
January 2024. Verify CHIVA ART dosing table for paediatric updates. OD=once daily, BD=twice daily
Standard adult regimen: TDF/FTC (Truvada®) 1 tablet OD + DTG (Dolutegravir) 50mg OD for 28 days. Start ASAP, ideally within 4 hours, not after 72 hours. Check renal function before TDF/FTC.
Dolutegravir (DTG) — Preferred integrase inhibitor
Adult (>20kg): 50mg OD | 14–19kg: 40mg OD | Dispersible (≥4 wks): 3–5kg 5mg OD | 6–9kg 15mg OD | 10–13kg 20mg OD | 14–19kg 25mg OD | ≥20kg 30mg OD
Side effects: Nausea, rash, sleep disturbance. NOTE: formulations not bioequivalent — specify when prescribing.
TDF/FTC (Truvada®) — Tenofovir Disoproxil / Emtricitabine
≥35kg: 1 tablet OD (TD 245mg / FTC 200mg)
Side effects: Headache, diarrhoea, nausea, renal tubular dysfunction. DO NOT USE if known renal impairment.
TAF/FTC (Descovy®) — Alternative to TDF/FTC
Licensed ≥12 yrs or ≥35kg: FTC 200mg/TAF 25mg OD with RAL or DTG
Side effects: Nausea. Preferred over TDF/FTC in renal impairment.
Raltegravir (RAL) — Alternative integrase inhibitor
≥40kg: 1200mg OD (2×600mg) or 400mg BD | Chewable (not bioequivalent): 11–13kg 75mg BD | 14–19kg 100mg BD | 20–27kg 150mg BD | 28–39kg 200mg BD | ≥40kg 300mg BD
Side effects: Rash, nausea, hepatitis. Specify formulation when prescribing.
Kaletra® (LPV/RTV) — Paediatric protease inhibitor
Adult (≥35kg): 2 tabs BD | 10–13kg: 2AM+1PM | 14–24kg: 2BD | 25–34kg: 3BD | Liquid (80/20 per mL): 3–5kg 1ml BD | 6–9kg 1.5ml BD | 10–13kg 2ml BD
Side effects: Diarrhoea, abdominal pain, nausea, vomiting, headache.
